Executive Summary

Northpine Industries operates as an engineer-to-order industrial manufacturer and scores 2.3/5.0 (Aware) on the PPDT maturity baseline. The Data pillar at 1.5 is capping the full system — no matter how strong People, Process, or Technology score, portfolio decisions cannot become data-driven until product master data integrity is resolved.

Product portfolio renewal decisions are currently made without verified product-level profitability data, exposing the business to retaining low-margin SKUs and under-investing in genuine high-margin opportunities.

Strongest pillar — People (2.8): A dedicated portfolio steering committee exists and leadership openly acknowledges its capability gaps; the willingness to act is a real asset.

Critical bottleneck — Data (1.5): Three days of manual reconciliation are required before leadership can review profitability; no single product master exists; no accountable data owner is named.

Immediate priority: Appoint a single Data Owner for product master data with a 90-day mandate to consolidate the three profitability sources into one verified view.

⚠ BOTTLENECK IDENTIFIED — DATA

The Data pillar scores 1.5/5.0 (Ad Hoc) and is the binding constraint on overall maturity. It caps People, Process and Technology because every portfolio decision ultimately requires a verified profitability view — and that view does not currently exist as a single source. The day-to-day impact is concrete: leadership reviews profitability four to six working days after month-end, on figures assembled manually across three systems, with no agreed product-master schema underneath. This means renewal, retirement and investment-reallocation decisions are made on numbers that nobody owns and nobody trusts. The single highest-leverage corrective action is to appoint a named Data Owner for product master data with a 90-day mandate to consolidate the three sources into one verified view.

Decision Risk

Portfolio renewal decisions are currently being made without product-level profitability data, meaning low-margin SKUs may be retained in the catalogue while genuine high-margin opportunities are under-invested due to incomplete visibility — and the gap will widen with every new project Northpine accepts.

Bottleneck principle: Hannila et al. (2022) — five preconditions that must all function together for data-driven PPM to be possible.
Improvement Roadmap

Actions are sequenced bottleneck-first. Phase 2 work will not improve overall maturity until the Phase 1 Data bottleneck is resolved.

Phase 1 · 0–3 Months

Resolve the Bottleneck

—Appoint a named Data Owner for product master data, reporting to the COO, with a 90-day mandate.
—Consolidate the three profitability data sources (ERP, CRM, Excel) into a unified product-level view — weekly refresh by month 3.
—Define and enforce one canonical SKU attribute schema across ERP, configurator and BOMs.
Projected: DATA score 1.5 → 2.8
Phase 2 · 3–12 Months

Systemic Improvement

—Establish a quarterly portfolio review cadence with documented retirement and reallocation criteria (governance).
—Roll out portfolio decision training for all eight product line and commercial leads.
—Integrate the configurator with the product master so engineering changes propagate automatically — eliminate the manual reconciliation FTE (data ownership).
Projected overall: 2.3 → 3.4
Phase 3 · 12+ Months

Sustained Capability

—Connect Power BI directly to the unified product master for real-time portfolio profitability dashboards.
—Introduce predictive analytics on portfolio mix using historical margin and configuration-complexity data.
—Consolidate governance under a Portfolio Council with charter, KPIs and quarterly board reporting.
Target maturity: Managed (4.0 / 5.0)

Each phase action must have a named governance owner. Investing in Phase 3 tools before the Phase 1 bottleneck is resolved will not improve overall portfolio maturity. Sequence matters.

Roadmap logic: Hannila et al. (2020), Journal of Enterprise Information Management, 33(1), 214–237. Bottleneck-first sequencing principle.
Portfolio Decision Impact
Current Decision Quality

Portfolio renewal and SKU rationalisation decisions are currently possible only at annual budget cadence and rely on profitability figures assembled manually across three systems. Customer revenue data exists; product cost data exists; the link between them — at SKU level — does not. Northpine is therefore unknowingly carrying low-margin SKUs in the catalogue and under-investing in genuine high-margin opportunities.

At Next Maturity Level

Once Phase 1 is complete and one verified product master exists, leadership will be able to review SKU-level profitability monthly rather than annually. Retirement decisions become evidence-based rather than political. The 0.5-FTE manual reconciliation effort is freed for analytical work, and month-end portfolio reporting cycle time drops from 6 days to under 2.

At Full Maturity (Level 4–5)

For an ETO industrial manufacturer of Northpine's profile, full maturity routinely supports continuous portfolio optimisation: live margin dashboards by product family, predictive flags for SKUs trending below threshold, and integrated configuration-complexity scoring at the moment of quote. The Portfolio Council reviews exception cases weekly rather than the entire portfolio annually.

Decision quality framing: Hannila (2019) and Hannila et al. (2022).
